# Keywords - Urgent Vet Campaign

## Campaign

Search - Urgent Vet Care - Edmonds

## Purpose

This file defines the approved keyword strategy for the urgent vet campaign.

The campaign should capture nearby pet owners searching for urgent veterinary help, sick pet visits, open-now care, and same-day availability.

The campaign should not become a general vet campaign.

---

## Approved Match Type Strategy

Use only:

- Exact match
- Phrase match

Do not use:

- Broad match
- Dynamic Search Ads
- AI-generated keyword expansion
- Google auto-applied keyword suggestions

---

```text
"animal hospital open now"

```

---

## Emergency-Adjacent Keywords

Use these carefully.

These can bring strong intent, but they may also attract users looking for a true 24/7 emergency hospital.

Only keep these if the clinic is comfortable receiving emergency-adjacent calls and the ad copy does not overpromise.

```text
"emergency vet near me"
[emergency vet near me]
"emergency animal hospital"
[emergency animal hospital]
"animal emergency hospital near me"
[animal emergency hospital near me]
"pet emergency vet"
```

---

## Watch Keywords

These terms may bring useful traffic, but they are broader and may eventually belong in the future General Vet campaign.

Do not block them immediately unless they spend money without calls or lead quality.

```text
"veterinary clinic near me"
"animal hospital near me"
"pet hospital near me"
"vet near me"
"veterinarian near me"
```

## Keyword Rules

### Keep

Keep keywords that clearly show urgent or immediate need:

```text
urgent
sick pet
open now
same day
animal hospital open now
emergency-adjacent
```

### Watch

Watch keywords that are useful but broader:

```text
vet near me
veterinary clinic near me
animal hospital near me
pet hospital near me
```

### Move Later

Move general intent keywords into a separate General Vet campaign once available.

```text
dog vet near me
cat vet near me
veterinarian near me
local vet near me
best vet near me
```

### Add Negative

Add negatives for terms that show non-client, competitor, or far-location intent.

Examples:

```text
shelter
adoption
rescue
foster
free
cheap
jobs
career
school
training
grooming
boarding
blue pearl
vca
banfield
far city names
```

## Search-Term Decision Rules

When reviewing search terms, classify each term as one of:

- Keep
- Watch
- Add negative
- Competitor
- Far-location
- Move to General Vet campaign
- Move to Services campaign

## Far-Location Watchlist

For urgent vet care, far-location traffic is usually low quality because pet owners usually need nearby help.

Watch and block if they generate clicks or repeated irrelevant impressions:

```text
spokane
spokane valley
olympia
renton
kirkland
poulsbo
issaquah
lake stevens
```

Only block additional cities when supported by search-term data.

---

## Important Notes

Do not overreact to impressions alone.

Prioritize action when a search term has:

- Clicks
- Spend
- Repeated impressions with poor intent
- Competitor intent
- Far-location intent
- Non-client intent

Do not block useful urgent intent just because the term is broad.

Do not add broad match.

Do not accept Google’s automated keyword expansion without review.

Do not expand the keyword list aggressively until the first 48 to 72 hours of data has been reviewed.

---

## Current Strategy

The urgent campaign should stay tight and controlled.

The goal is not maximum impressions.

The goal is qualified urgent pet-owner traffic that can become calls, appointments, and real clinic visits.
