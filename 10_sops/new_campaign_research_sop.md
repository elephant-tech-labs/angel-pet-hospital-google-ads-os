# New Campaign Research SOP

## Purpose

This SOP defines how to research, approve, and prepare any new Google Ads campaign before launch.

The goal is to avoid guessing and prevent wasteful campaign creation.

---

## Applies To

Use this SOP for:

- General Vet
- Brand
- Dental
- Surgery
- Wellness / Vaccines
- Promotions
- Remarketing
- Future Performance Max

---

## Campaign Research Workflow

### Step 1: Create Campaign Folder

Create a campaign folder under:

```text
02_campaigns/
```

Example:

```text
02_campaigns/general_vet/
```

---

### Step 2: Add Required Files

Each campaign folder should include:

```text
campaign_brief.md
research_brief.md
keyword_research.md
competitor_research.md
landing_page_brief.md
launch_recommendation.md
keywords.md
ads.md
assets.md
negatives.md
optimization_notes.md
```

Some files may start as placeholders.

---

### Step 3: Define Campaign Purpose

The campaign brief must answer:

- What intent does this campaign capture?
- What business goal does it support?
- What is the primary CTA?
- What landing page is needed?
- What should not be mixed into this campaign?
- What budget is recommended?
- What tracking is required?

---

### Step 4: Research Existing Account Data

Use Codex with Google Ads MCP to review:

- current search terms
- current keyword performance
- existing negatives
- location data
- device data
- campaign overlap
- conversion data
- landing page data

---

### Step 5: Research Demand

Use one or more:

- Google Ads Keyword Planner export
- search term data
- competitor ads
- SERP screenshots
- client service priorities
- clinic margin / service value

If data is missing, request it instead of guessing.

---

### Step 6: Build Keyword Themes

Group keywords by intent.

Example:

```text
Vet Near Me
Animal Hospital
Dog Vet
Cat Vet
New Patient Vet
```

Do not mix unrelated intent in one ad group.

---

### Step 7: Define Negatives

Check:

- global negatives
- campaign-specific negatives
- competitor negatives
- service overlap negatives
- far-location negatives

Avoid overblocking.

---

### Step 8: Define Landing Page

Do not launch full campaigns to the homepage long-term.

Each major campaign should have a dedicated landing page or a clearly relevant page.

---

### Step 9: Launch Recommendation

Before launch, update:

```text
launch_recommendation.md
```

with one of:

```text
Ready to build
Needs more research
Needs landing page first
Needs tracking first
Needs client approval
Do not launch
```

---

### Step 10: Strategy Director Review

Codex research should be reviewed by the strategy director before implementation.

Do not launch only because Codex recommends it.

---

### Step 11: Manual Build

Campaigns are built manually in Google Ads.

Do not use auto-generated campaign setup blindly.

---

### Step 12: Pre-Launch QA

Run Codex post-change or pre-launch QA before enabling the campaign.

---

### Step 13: Post-Launch Monitoring

For the first 7 days:

- review search terms daily
- check spend pacing
- check calls/forms/bookings
- add negatives
- check disapprovals
- check settings drift
- do not scale prematurely

---

## Launch Gate

Before launch, all must pass:

| Requirement | Status |
|---|---|
| Campaign brief complete | Required |
| Keyword research complete | Required |
| Keywords approved | Required |
| Negatives approved | Required |
| Ads approved | Required |
| Landing page exists or approved | Required |
| Tracking plan exists | Required |
| Budget approved | Required |
| Pre-launch QA passed | Required |

---

## Final Rule

No new campaign should be launched from guesswork.

Every campaign should be supported by:

```text
strategy
research
keyword map
negative logic
ad copy
landing page plan
tracking plan
QA
```
